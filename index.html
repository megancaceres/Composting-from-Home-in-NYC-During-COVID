Here's a Quick Start Guide to Composting at Home:

Select suitable equipment for urban composting, and choose a composting bin that caters to your lifestyle. Such varieties include:A metal can compost bin, an earth machine compost bin, an eco master 300 compost bin, or a worm bin. If you choose a worm bin, you will need to get red wiggler worms to put inside.
Ensure that compost bins are strictly vegetarian, and should only include items of organic origin, meaning, plants or plant based materials. The only exception is eggshells, hair or fur.
Add sticks and leaves (carbon), to prevent the compost from getting slimy.
Use the Lasagna Layering method to ensure a successful compost. Lasagna Layering allows you to layer the right amounts of air, water, and food, in order to produce a rich compost. Start by setting up sticks at the base for air. Add a layer of "browns" (leaves), which will act as the carbon base. Then, add your layer of food scraps (greens), which will act as the Nitrogen base.Cover the food scraps with a layer of browns and repeat this till it is layered to your liking.
Optional step: Turning speeds up the comoposting process. Use a shovel and or rake to toss and turn the compost.
Monitor your bin! If it no longer looks like recognizable food, and the stuff at the bottom of the bin looks and smells like earth, then congratulations, you have successfully composted and created soil for your plants!
